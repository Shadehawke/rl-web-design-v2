The web has an accessibility problem.

87% of websites have detectable accessibility issues. The number is higher for design-forward sites — dark themes with poor contrast, decorative fonts on body copy, no keyboard navigation, animations that ignore prefers-reduced-motion.

Atmosphere and accessibility are treated as trade-offs. They aren't. Every site I build is designed to look intentional and work for everyone — keyboard users, screen readers, people on slow connections, and clients who want a beautiful site that actually loads.

  • WCAG-informed markup
  • Keyboard navigable
  • Reduced-motion aware
  • Real contrast ratios

About

At Raven's Loft Web Design, I believe every website should be more than a collection of pages — it should be an experience where creativity, clarity, and identity converge.

My approach is rooted in balance: form and function, art and accessibility, imagination and usability. I craft sites that are visually striking yet easy to navigate, designed to resonate with audiences while reflecting the unique character of the brand they represent.

That means dark themes with contrast ratios that actually pass. Animations that respect motion preferences. Keyboard navigation that works. Performance that doesn't sacrifice atmosphere to get there.
